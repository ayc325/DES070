Is it Finished?

Finish, done, and complete seems so easy to say when talking about class assignments or any progressable work. Reality is, nothing is finished and nothing will ever be complete or done right. Humans are constantly evolving and coming up with new ways to improve themselves, past works, and creations. They're constantly given chances and this leads to nothing ever being finished. Some “never-finished” people that are to be spoken of include students, athletes, scientists, writers, and web designers.

Students usually “complete” an assignment, but is it really complete? Going back and editing it, referring back to the assignment for other assignments, or using it to study can all be ways to show that nothing is ever “finished.” Students are never finished.

Athletes may finish a race but they're always seeking improvement and striving for their ultimate best. On this note, if an athlete is striving for their best and reaching their limits, are there even limits? Athletes are never finished.

Scientists have learned so much and have met several milestones, yet they are still striving to reach higher and further to discover more. Scientists are never finished. In fact, this story here doesn't have an end. It keeps going endlessly with surprises and other people's work. With every author writing several works of writing, if they were all connected to each other, one's writing is never finished.

Web Design is another industry where nothing is ever finished. Although web designers who code using HTML and CSS are on the dying side, web designers will always be needed. No single web project is ever finished in the real world. Web designers continue to manage and maintain the websites and webpages for their personal sites, companies, institutions, and organizations. Web designers are never finished.

As seen here, there are occupations with tasks, duties and responsibilities that will never be complete. These are just a few, and there are many more out there. Although small tasks may be “finished,” long term, “finishing” is just a concept that we humans try to embed in our heads to give ourselves a sense of accomplishment and pleasure.
